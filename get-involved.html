We believe in an open-source world and sharing.

GET INVOLVED!

We strive to develop effective and affordable systems and globally useful designs and knowledge, making them open-source open-hardware for everyone. Our goal is to empower the world with technologies that improve lives, let that be an advanced communication system or a simple everyday utensil. We are non-profit, rewarding the ones that create, always exploring new projects and ideas.

If you share the same vision and want to contribute, we invite you to get involved with IRNAS and our projects, either on the commercial or nonprofit side.

MAKE / BUY

If you want to make our systems you can 3D print the majority of parts yourself, others should be readily available on the market. You can also pre-order DIY KIT or preasembled units from our partner company Fabricor.

KORUZA DIY friendly open-source release together with a complete kit of assembly parts as well as full system documentation and detailed instructions on how to properly assemble and test the system is already available. Check KORUZA source documentation here.

GoodEnoughCNC DIY friendly open-source release together with a complete kit of assembly parts is in final stages of release and will be available in next months. CAD files and technical documentation for individual build are already available in a dedicated GitHub repository.

DONATE

We strive to develop globally useful designs and knowledge and make them open-source open-hardware for everyone. This would not be possible without the support of the global community and we are very thankful for everyone supporting us.

To support our full-time work we appeal to everyone finding our work useful and more importantly being used for profit, to financially support us.

Raised funds go towards operational costs, salaries and creation of prototypes. Your support enables us to continue the work and come up with new projects.

Work with us!

Our team is based in Maribor, Slovenia and vacancies are primarily aimed at locals as we wish to optimize the creative interaction between the team, but anyone from anywhere is welcome to apply. The below listed positions are currently open, however at any time you are welcome to get in touch via email if you would like to collaborate with us.

We are looking for a full-time electronics developer to work on open source open hardware systems for optical communications, control systems and other embedded devices, working primarily with Arduino environment, ARM devices and CPLD/FPGA systems.
